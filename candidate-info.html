About the Project

What is the Human Factor at Work?

We spend one third of our waking week at work for fifty years of our lives. Yet how often does our workplace genuinely support our psychological health — the inner experience of stress, purpose, balance, and belonging?

The Human Factor at Work is a pioneering project run by Leicester Vaughan College (LVC), funded by the NEBOSH Social Purpose Fund. Over Year 1, we are working with employers and workers across Leicester's private, public, and voluntary sectors to develop courses, workshops, and community spaces that address the real human experience of working life.

This is not a compliance programme. It is not a tick-box exercise. It is a space to think honestly about what work does to people — and what people can do about it, individually and together.

The project covers the full working life: from preparing for your first job, starting out in a new role, managing stress and caring responsibilities, navigating career transitions and organisational change, developing as a manager — through to preparing for retirement.

Underpinning Concepts

The ideas behind the Human Factor at Work

Five interlocking concepts underpin everything we do. Understanding these will help you get the most from any course, workshop, or Crossroads Café session you attend.

Psychological Health

Strongly linked to capacity to innovate. By prioritising psychological health, organisations not only create a more positive and productive environment — they build a loyal workforce that champions the organisation both internally and externally.

The Emotionally Intelligent Organisation

An organisation that understands, manages, and uses emotions — both individual and collective — to create a healthy, productive, and humane workplace. It treats emotional dynamics not as "soft stuff" but as core infrastructure for performance, trust, and wellbeing. In practice: people feel safe, respected, understood, and able to do their best work without fear or pretence.

Individual, Team & Organisational Development

At all three levels, human beings have immense capacity to realise their potential. Understanding limiting factors and finding collaborative ways of addressing them is the core work of development. Emotions at work are shaped by: motivation around the organisation's purpose; its systems, practices and culture; and events in the lives of individual employees, inside and outside work.

Adult Learning

We draw on the traditions of adult education — learning that is participatory, experience-led, and built on the principle that adults bring rich knowledge to any learning situation. Our approach combines core concepts with creative reflection, peer support, and practical application to your own working life.

Co-operative Values

As a cooperative, LVC works to values that include: caring for others, involvement in decision-making, equity, equality, honesty, openness, self-help, self-responsibility, solidarity, and social responsibility. These values shape how we deliver, not just what we deliver.

A Core Concept

Psychological Safety — four stages

Psychological safety is one of the most researched concepts in workplace wellbeing. It is defined not as a single thing, but as a progression through four stages — each building on the last. Our courses address all four levels.

1
Inclusion Safety

Feeling safe to be yourself — belonging without having to mask who you are or where you come from.

2
Learner Safety

Feeling safe to learn — to ask questions, make mistakes, experiment, and develop without fear of humiliation.

3
Contributor Safety

Feeling safe to contribute — to share your ideas, skills, and perspectives and have them taken seriously.

4
Challenger Safety

Feeling safe to challenge — to raise concerns, question the status quo, and advocate for change without fear of reprisal.

What this means in practice

A psychologically safe workplace is one where people feel safe, respected, understood, and able to do their best work without fear or pretence. Our courses help both individuals and organisations understand where they are on this spectrum — and what to do about it.

Our Approach

How we teach and learn

Every course and workshop combines four elements — so that learning is grounded in real experience, practically useful, and genuinely supportive.

🧠
Core concepts

Exploring and testing key ideas from psychological health research in relation to your own working situation — making theory practical and relevant.

✍️
Creative reflection

Creative exercises for reflexivity — helping you understand your own experience more clearly and apply new thinking to your specific situation.

🤝
Peer support skills

Building the capacity to support colleagues — listening, mutuality, and the skills of genuine peer connection — alongside your own development.

📚
Resources & materials

Access to print, video, and digital resources for reinforcement and more in-depth investigation — including the project newsletter and resource bank.

Delivery formats
🏢
In-house

Delivered within your organisation — tailored to your team, your sector, your specific challenges. We come to you.

👥
Composite cohort

Mixed groups of 1–4 people from different organisations in a cohort of approximately 16 — rich cross-sector peer learning.

🌐
Open access

Open to individuals — no employer needed. In-person, online, and hybrid options. Evening and Saturday sessions to fit working life.

Community Space

The Crossroads Café

The Crossroads Café is an informal peer support space — no agenda, no certificates, no formal structure. Just honest conversation about the real experience of work, with people who understand.

It is open to anyone — workers, managers, freelancers, carers in employment, people navigating change. You do not need to have attended any other part of the project to join.

The Crossroads Café is part of a broader network and community of practice that the project will build — contributing to Leicester becoming a genuinely good place to work and live.

Getting Involved
