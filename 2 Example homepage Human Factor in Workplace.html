About the Project

What is the Human Factor at Work?

We spend one third of our waking week at work for fifty years of our lives. Yet how often does our workplace genuinely support our psychological health — the inner experience of stress, purpose, balance, and belonging?

The Human Factor at Work is a pioneering project run by Leicester Vaughan College, funded by the NEBOSH Social Purpose Fund. Over Year 1, we are engaging employers and workers across Leicester's private, public, and voluntary sectors to develop courses, workshops, and community spaces that address the real human experience of working life.

This is not a compliance programme. It is not a tick-box exercise. It is a space to think honestly about what work does to people — and what people can do about it, individually and together.

The project covers the full working life course: preparing for work, starting a new job, managing stress and workload, balancing caring responsibilities, navigating career transitions, managing teams, and preparing for retirement.

Who is this for?

This project is for everyone at work

Whether you are an individual worker, a manager, an employer, or someone navigating work on your own terms — there is something here for you.

🧑‍💼
Individual workers

Anyone in any sector navigating the realities of working life — stress, change, relationships at work, life balance, transitions, and finding meaning in what you do.

👥
Managers & team leaders

New to management or experienced — developing the skills to lead in psychologically safe ways, support your team's wellbeing, and handle difficult situations with confidence.

🏢
Employers & organisations

Private, public, and third sector employers wanting to build genuinely healthy workplace cultures — beyond HR policy, into lived experience and real change.

🛠️
Sole traders & freelancers

Working for yourself brings unique pressures — isolation, uncertainty, the blurring of work and life. This project explicitly includes those working outside traditional employment.

Workshop spotlight
Balancing work and caring responsibilities
Targeted workshop — open access & in-house
1
Name what you are carrying. Many carers do not identify as carers — which makes it harder to ask for the support that exists. The first step is naming your situation honestly.
2
Know your rights. UK law provides rights to flexible working, emergency leave, and Carer's Leave. Many workers do not know they exist. We cover these practically.
3
Set sustainable limits — not just limits. Sustainable boundaries are ones you can maintain. We explore what that looks like in your specific work context.
4
Build a peer support network. Others in your workplace or sector are carrying similar loads. Connection reduces isolation and builds resilience.

About Us

Rooted in Leicester

Leicester Vaughan College is an independent higher education college established as a co-operative Community Benefit Society. Founded in 2017, our mission is to champion adult learning in Leicester and Leicestershire as a public good.

The Human Factor at Work is the most ambitious funded project we have undertaken — and it reflects everything our cooperative values stand for: caring for others, solidarity, equity, and building community through learning.

We aim to contribute to Leicester being not just a place where people work, but a genuinely good place to work and live.

Co-operative values: caring for others, solidarity, equity, openness, self-help and social responsibility
Community benefit: this project serves Leicester and Leicestershire — all sectors, all career stages
Peer learning: knowledge is built together, not delivered from above
